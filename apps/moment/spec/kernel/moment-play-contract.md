# Moment Play Contract - MM-PLAY-*

> Short continuation loop after the story opening appears.

## MM-PLAY-001: Short Loop Only

Moment supports a short continuation loop, not an endless session shell.

The target interaction window is 2 to 4 beats per story opening.

## MM-PLAY-002: Dynamic Relation State

User relation to the unfolding story is dynamic state, not a fixed role selected before play begins.

The loop must allow the relationship to:

- remain distant
- move closer
- become noticed
- become addressed
- become involved

if and only if the scene and the user's actions make that development feel earned.

## MM-PLAY-003: Distinct Ways In

The three opening actions must feel semantically distinct.

They should imply different stances toward the moment rather than minor wording variants of the same move.

## MM-PLAY-004: The User May Stay Outside The Story

The continuation loop may place the user:

- inside the unfolding event
- at the edge of the unfolding event
- or outside it as a witness

All of these are valid as long as the opening and the next beats make the relationship legible.
The product must not force user involvement when continued observation is the more compelling path.

## MM-PLAY-005: Freeform User Line

After the story opening appears, the user must be able to continue with a short custom line in addition to choosing one of the provided actions.

## MM-PLAY-006: Scene Identity Must Hold

Continuation must preserve the identity of the story opening.

The product must not lose the source scene, collapse into generic chat, or drift into unrelated prose after the first reply.

## MM-PLAY-007: Each Beat Must Open The Story Further

Each continuation beat must help the user see further into what may be happening inside the scene.

The loop must not simply restate the same uncertainty or stall in abstract atmosphere.
It should make the possible story feel slightly more tangible without collapsing it into full explanation too early.

## MM-PLAY-008: Relation Changes Must Feel Earned

When the story shifts from ignoring the user to noticing, addressing, or involving them, that shift must feel motivated by:

- the scene's own logic
- the current story tension
- the user's approach or intervention

It must not feel arbitrary or mechanically forced for the sake of excitement.

## MM-PLAY-009: Entering The Moment Remains Primary

Replay, variation, and continuation controls may exist, but the first experience priority is always entering the story opening.

The user should feel invited inward, not redirected into a tool workflow.

## MM-PLAY-010: Sealed Before Over-Explanation

The loop should seal while the story opening still feels charged.

Moment should stop before it turns into a complete story explanation or a long-form roleplay session.

## MM-PLAY-011: Sealing Window

The short loop must enter a sealing window before it becomes a long conversation.

In practice:

- the loop may deepen for a few beats
- by the later beats it should begin writing toward a memorable stopping point
- it should not keep opening fresh subplots just to justify one more turn

## MM-PLAY-012: Soft Stop, Not Hard Cut

Moment should stop like a camera finding the right final frame, not like a system interrupt.

A good ending beat should make the user feel:

- this moment is complete enough to keep
- there is still aftertaste
- the story has not been over-explained

The product must avoid abrupt mechanical endings such as:

- hard max-turn error messages
- blunt "session ended" language
- obvious system-imposed interruption text

## MM-PLAY-013: Continue Is Not Always Primary

Once the moment reaches the sealing window, the product should begin favoring graceful closure over endless continuation.

The shell may still allow one more step inward, but it must also make room for:

- letting the moment stop here
- or starting a new moment

without making that choice feel like failure or abandonment.

## MM-PLAY-014: Continuity Beats Surprise

Continuation must preserve narrative consistency across beats.

The loop must not casually contradict or overwrite:

- the established scene layout
- time-of-day or atmospheric conditions
- what objects were already present
- what the user already did
- the emotional lane already established

The product should prefer coherent development over surprise escalation.

Adding a new object, clue, or symbolic prop is allowed only when it feels like a plausible extension of what was already there, not a fresh invention added to force intrigue.
